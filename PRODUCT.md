# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Municipal staff of the Ville and CPAS de Marche-en-Famenne (a Belgian commune).
They open the intranet during the workday from office desktops (and occasionally
phones/tablets) to catch up on internal news, find documents, see colleagues'
birthdays, browse classified ads, and jump into the Filament `/app` admin tools.
French-speaking. Mixed digital comfort: from power users to occasional visitors.

## Product Purpose

A single internal home page that surfaces what matters to staff today (news,
people, documents, activities, RSS/press) and routes them into the deeper
intranet modules. Success is staff getting to the thing they need in a glance,
without training, and feeling the workplace is connected and current.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy and institutional, but warm and human. Efficient and scannable
first; modern and polished so it never reads as a dated legacy intranet. Voice
is plain, direct French. The page should feel like a well-run public service:
calm, reliable, people-first.

## Anti-references

- Dated gov / legacy intranet (cramped tables, gray-on-gray, early-2010s
  SharePoint feel).
- Loud consumer / marketing site (gradient-everywhere, oversized hero, salesy
  energy).
- Sterile corporate SaaS (generic blue-and-white template that feels
  impersonal).
- Cluttered / overwhelming dashboards where many cards and widgets compete for
  attention at once.

## Design Principles

- **Glance before click.** Every section answers "what's new / who / what" in
  one scan; depth lives one click away in the modules.
- **Warm institution.** Reliability and officialness carried by structure and
  restraint; warmth carried by people content and typography, not by louder
  color.
- **One thing leads.** A clear primary focus per region; avoid equal-weight card
  grids that flatten hierarchy.
- **Quiet polish.** Motion and decoration are subtle and purposeful, never the
  point.
- **Inclusive by default.** Readable for mixed digital comfort and assistive
  tech.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text >=4.5:1 contrast, large text >=3:1, full keyboard
navigation, visible focus states, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for
every animation (the home page already animates fade-in-up / float / card-hover).
